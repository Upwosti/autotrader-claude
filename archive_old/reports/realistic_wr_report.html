Why the Win Rate Was Fake (80%+ = Unrealistic)

🐛 Bug 1 — WR Lock-In Loop (Primary Cause)

Once XAUUSD reached 86.2% WR, the acceptance floor was set to 86.2% × 0.90 = 77.6%. This meant the system could never again accept any result below 77.6% WR — even if a 60% WR / 2.5 RRR strategy had much higher expectancy (E=1.175R vs E=0.615R at 86%/0.9R).

Effect: System locked into WR-farming. Better strategies were permanently blocked.

Fix: Replaced best_wr × 0.90 floor with a flat realistic band: 55% – 75%. Score (expectancy-first) determines acceptance, not WR alone.

🐛 Bug 2 — Tight SL on Daily Bars

The best XAUUSD params used sl_atr_mult = 0.75 — the SL was placed just 0.75×ATR below entry on a daily bar. A typical D1 ATR for XAUUSD is $15-20. With a $11-15 SL, intraday wicks would frequently touch the SL but the candle closes above it. The backtester uses bar OHLC — it does check the bar's low against the SL — but: when the daily candle closes above SL (after an intraday wick), the trade stays open. This means many real-life stop-outs are not captured.

Fix: SL range changed from [0.3, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.5] to [1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0]. Realistic D1 SL is 1.5-2.5×ATR to survive intraday noise.

🐛 Bug 3 — TP/SL Same-Bar Always Assumed TP Won

When a daily candle's high hits the TP level and the low hits the SL level (both in the same bar), the old code always resolved it as a win. In reality, the outcome depends on which price was hit first during the trading day.

Fix: Use bar direction to determine order:

  • Bullish bar (close ≥ open) → TP likely hit first → Win
  • Bearish bar (close < open) → SL likely hit first → Loss
